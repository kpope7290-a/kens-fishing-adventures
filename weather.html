Weather & Moon Phases

Fishing isn’t just about luck — the moon and the weather play a big role in how fish bite. Different phases of the moon change feeding patterns, and knowing when fish are most active can save you hours of waiting. Anglers often notice stronger bites during new and full moons, while waning moons can make fish sluggish. Paying attention to moon cycles helps you plan trips when the odds are in your favor.

Moon Phases & Fish Bite Activity

Moon Phase Fish Bite Info Best Species
🌑 New Moon Fish feed hard, especially at night. 🐟 Salmon, 🎣 Trout
🌓 Waxing Moon Steady bites during the day. 🐟 Perch, 🎣 Bass
🌕 Full Moon Strong feeding windows, especially at night. 🐟 Catfish, 🎣 Walleye
🌗 Waning Moon Bites slow down, fish hang deeper. 🐟 Deep‑water Trout

Air Pressure & Fish Biting

Air pressure is another factor that makes a big difference in fishing success. Fish have swim bladders that react to pressure changes, so they feed differently depending on the weather. Rising pressure usually means steady bites, while falling pressure can trigger short feeding frenzies before storms. Low pressure often slows everything down, pushing fish deeper and making them harder to catch.

Pressure Trend Barometric Range (inHg) Fish Behavior Best Species
⬆️ Rising Pressure 30.1 – 30.4 Fish are active, steady feeding. 🐟 Perch, 🎣 Bass
➡️ Stable Pressure 29.9 – 30.1 Normal feeding activity, predictable bites. 🐟 Bluegill, 🎣 Crappie
⬇️ Falling Pressure 29.5 – 29.9 Short feeding window before storms. 🐟 Salmon, 🎣 Trout
⚠️ Low Pressure 29.2 and below Fish retreat deeper, sluggish bites. 🐟 Catfish (near bottom)
