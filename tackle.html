Main Species & Recommended Gear

Species Rod/Reel Type Bait & Lures Hook Size
Yellow Perch Light spinning rod/reel Small jigs, minnows, soft plastics #6 – #8
Coho & Chinook Salmon Medium-heavy rod, trolling reel Spoons, crankbaits, trolling rigs 1/0 – 3/0
Steelhead & Brown Trout Medium spinning rod/reel Spawn sacs, inline spinners, slip bobber rigs #4 – #8
Smallmouth & Largemouth Bass Medium baitcaster/spinning rod Plastic worms, crankbaits, topwater lures #2 – 3/0 (depending on lure)
Bluegill & Crappie Ultralight spinning rod/reel Small jigs, waxworms, bobbers #8 – #12
Channel Catfish Heavy rod, baitcasting reel Stink bait, cut bait, chicken liver 2/0 – 5/0 circle hooks
Carp & Sheepshead Medium-heavy spinning rod Corn, dough balls, bottom rigs #2 – #6

Seasonal Gear

  • 🌨 Winter: Sunflower propane heater, hand warmers, insulated gloves, ice cleats
  • ☀️ Summer: Bug spray, sunscreen, wide‑brim hat, polarized sunglasses
  • 🍂 Fall/Spring: Rain jacket, layered clothing, waterproof boots

Tackle Essentials

  • Pliers & line cutters
  • Sharp knife
  • Spare hooks (#6 – 3/0)
  • Sinkers & split shot
  • Bobbers & floats
  • Extra line

Useful Fishing Knots

  • Improved Clinch Knot – secure hook or lure
  • Palomar Knot – strong and simple for braided line
  • Loop Knot – gives lures more action
  • Uni Knot – versatile for hooks, swivels, and line joining
