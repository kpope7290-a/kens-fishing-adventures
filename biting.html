What’s Biting in Chicago

To be a successful Angler you have to know what fish to target and when to go after them so were giving you a cheat sheet of some of the basics. Seasonal guide to fish activity, water temps, and the best baits. Use this chart to plan your trips year‑round and maximize your chances of landing the perfect catch.

Season Species Activity Preferred Water Temp Best Baits
🌨 Winter (Dec–Feb) Yellow Perch Active in harbors 32–40°F Minnows, wax worms, small jigs
Walleye Bite well in cold rivers 35–45°F Jigging minnows, crankbaits
Steelhead Trout Found in tributaries 34–42°F Spawn sacs, spoons, spinners
🌱 Spring (Mar–May) Coho Salmon Peak runs in April–May 45–55°F Dodgers with flies, spoons
Brown Trout Active as temps rise 48–55°F Minnows, crankbaits, spinners
Smallmouth Bass Start biting at 50°F+ 50–60°F Soft plastics, jerkbaits
☀️ Summer (Jun–Aug) Catfish Strong in rivers/lagoons 65–75°F Stink bait, chicken liver, cut bait
Bluegill & Crappie Bite aggressively in warm ponds 70–80°F Worms, small jigs
Smallmouth Bass Thrive in rocky Lake Michigan areas 65–75°F Crayfish imitators, spinnerbaits
🍂 Fall (Sep–Nov) Chinook Salmon Peak runs in Sept–Oct 50–60°F Spoons, plugs, spawn sacs
Coho Salmon Continue biting late fall 48–55°F Trolling flies, crankbaits
Walleye Active in cooler waters 45–55°F Jigging minnows, blade baits
Steelhead Trout Strong bite in rivers 45–52°F Spawn sacs, spinners
